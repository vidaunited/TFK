# TFK — The Fragrance Kitchen: Brand Guidelines

*Working guidelines for marketing, content, e-commerce, and communications. To be refined with official assets from the brand team.*

---

## 1. Brand Positioning

**One-line positioning:** TFK is Kuwait's luxury fragrance house — where Middle Eastern perfume heritage meets French perfumery, translating memory into scent.

**Brand pillars:**
1. **Heritage & Memory** — founded on the founder's childhood memories of Oud, Taif Rose, and burning Agarwood. Every story we tell can connect back to memory, family, and ritual.
2. **East Meets West** — Gulf perfume tradition refined through French perfumery craft. Wearable luxury, lighter than typical oud-heavy Gulf blends.
3. **Modern Luxury** — premium quality, immersive retail, elevated digital experience. "A touch of luxury in each bottle."
4. **Playful Sophistication** — witty fragrance names (War of the Roses, Musky Ever After, Palm Fiction, Fly Me to the Rose) keep the brand human and charming, never stiff.
5. **Lived-In Luxury** — newer messaging positions fragrance as daily ritual rather than precious occasion-wear: *"Three perfumes worn in rotation. Built to be lived in, not saved for occasions."* (from "The Sets" campaign). Use this anti-precious, everyday-wear framing for rotation/set products without undercutting the prestige framing used for Signature/Exclusive tiers.

## 2. Audience

- **Primary:** Affluent fragrance lovers in Kuwait and the GCC, 25–45, men and women (portfolio is largely unisex in spirit), who value niche/luxury scents over mass-market designer perfume.
- **Secondary:** International niche-fragrance enthusiasts discovering Middle Eastern perfumery; gift buyers (major occasions: Eid, Ramadan, National Day, weddings, graduations).
- **Entry customers:** Younger buyers entering via Discovery Sets (25 KWD), Mists (25 KWD), and the 35 KWD Everyday/Remix tier.

## 3. Voice & Tone

**Voice (always):** Elegant, warm, confident, sensorial. We speak like a gracious host inviting you into a sensory experience — never like a discount retailer.

**Tone by context:**
- Product storytelling → evocative and poetic ("Saffron rose with royal allure", "Oud elegance meets garden bloom")
- Social media → lighter, charming, still refined; wit is welcome, slang is not
- Customer service → gracious, personal, solution-first
- Promotions → understated luxury; prefer "complimentary", "curated", "discover" over "cheap", "deal", "sale blowout"

**Language:** English, Arabic, and French are all brand languages. Arabic content should carry the same elegance, not be a literal translation.

**Words we use:** curated, crafted, discover, journey, memory, heritage, signature, exclusive, complimentary, immersive, sensory.
**Words we avoid:** cheap, discount-heavy language, "perfume dupe" framing, aggressive urgency ("BUY NOW!!"), anything that undercuts luxury positioning.

## 4. Visual Identity (working notes)

- **Logo:** lowercase serif wordmark "tfk" (confirmed live on tfk.com.kw header and on bottle labels) — not all-caps. Used in black on light backgrounds and white on dark backgrounds. Always clear space, never stretched or recolored.
- **Palette direction:** Monochrome black/white foundation with warm luxury accents (amber, gold tones); product photography carries the color. *(Confirm exact hex values with the brand team / WAVE, the web developer.)*
- **Photography style:** Clean, editorial, product-forward; bottles as heroes; warm lighting; retail imagery leans on the immersive store concept (the bukhoor-smoke wooden wall is a signature visual).
- **Naming convention:** Fragrance names in ALL CAPS on product cards and packaging (ARAB SPRING, LA FEMME CAMELIA, BLACK EMBER).
- **Two packaging design languages confirmed on-site:**
  - *Signature/Exclusive tier* (e.g. After Silence) — matte black bottle, black cap, centered white ornate mandala emblem above the fragrance name, minimal and ritualistic.
  - *Sets/rotation tier* ("The Sets") — matte off-white/cream bottles, black cap, oversized abstract graphic typography unique per scent (bold overlapping letterforms/numerals in red, navy, silver) covering the label; more playful and collectible, distinct from the Signature bottle's restraint.
- **Service messaging on-site:** "Buy Now Pay Later Available Now in Kuwait" banner — add BNPL to approved service reassurances (see §6).

## 5. Product & Price Architecture (for consistent communication)

| Tier | Lines | Price (KWD) | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry / Discovery | Discovery Sets, Mists | 25 | Recruitment, gifting, trial |
| Everyday | The Finest Everyday, Remix line, Black Ember, Seventh Sense, etc. | 35 | Volume, daily wear |
| Signature | Core EDP line (100ml) | 60 | Core of the brand |
| Exclusive / Elevated | Exclusive Collection, L'Extase Blanche, etc. | 75 | Prestige |
| Curated trio sets | Rose Frequency, Quiet Bloom, etc. | 60–90 | Gifting, AOV growth |

Always present the Discovery Set as the start of the "fragrance journey" and the Signature/Exclusive lines as the destination.

## 6. Messaging Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Lead with story and scent notes, then price
- Anchor campaigns in memory, heritage, and craftsmanship
- Highlight the founder's story where it adds credibility
- Use "complimentary shipping", "secure payment", and "Buy Now Pay Later available in Kuwait" as service reassurances
- Respect gifting occasions in the Gulf calendar (Ramadan, Eid Al-Fitr, Eid Al-Adha, Kuwait National & Liberation Days in February, back-to-school/graduation, wedding season)

**Don't:**
- Compare TFK to designer mass-market brands or "dupes"
- Over-discount publicly; protect price integrity of the Signature and Exclusive tiers
- Use stock imagery that conflicts with the editorial photography style
- Mix collection names incorrectly (note: the *Signature Collection* uses the handle `exclusive-collection` and the *Exclusive Collection* uses `secret-collection` on the website — always verify which collection is meant before publishing links)

- **YouTube (@TheFragranceKitchen):** Long-form brand films, campaign content.
