# TFK — The Fragrance Kitchen: Brand Overview

## Who We Are

TFK, The Fragrance Kitchen, is a prestigious luxury fragrance house from Kuwait, founded by Sheikh Majed Al-Sabah and today part of the Development Holding Company (DH). The brand was born from memory: the scents of Sheikh Majed's upbringing, and in particular his grandmother's daily use of Oud, Taif Rose, and burning Agarwood. What began as personal blends made for friends and family grew into an internationally recognized fragrance house.

TFK's craft sits at the intersection of two worlds — the depth and ritual of Middle Eastern perfumery, and the technical refinement of French perfumery. Fragrances have been created in collaboration with renowned perfumers, and the house is known for East-meets-West compositions that are often lighter and more wearable than typical Gulf oud blends, with playful, memorable names (War of the Roses, Musky Ever After, Palm Fiction, Some Like It Bloom).

## The Founder's Story

Sheikh Majed Al-Sabah discovered his olfactory talent at an early age, encouraged by his grandmother to explore scent in the kitchen of the family home — the origin of the name "The Fragrance Kitchen." His career in luxury retail and fashion, including Villa Moda and collaborations with houses such as Gucci and Yves Saint Laurent, and the influence of figures like Tom Ford, shaped TFK's vision: fragrance as a translation of memory, presented with contemporary luxury standards.

## Brand Essence

- **Tagline / promise:** "A touch of luxury in each bottle" · "Your fragrance journey starts here"
- **Core idea:** Fragrance as memory — heritage ingredients (Oud, Taif Rose, Agarwood, saffron, amber) reinterpreted with modern elegance
- **Positioning:** Niche luxury fragrance house; Kuwaiti heritage with international credibility
- **Personality:** Luxurious yet approachable, heritage-rooted yet contemporary, refined with a playful wit (seen in fragrance naming)
